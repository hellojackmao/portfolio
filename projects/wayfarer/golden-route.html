Discover Korea

A golden-age sojourn through the palaces, peaks, and lacquered cities of Korea — where arriving is still treated as an occasion worth dressing for.

There was an age when travel was an art — when one did not merely go to a place, but arrived, beautifully, having anticipated it for months. The Golden Route restores that art to Korea: a country of gilded temple eaves and mother-of-pearl moonlight, presented not as a checklist, but as a grand and glittering occasion.

The Treasures · A Gilded Gallery

Five Splendours of Korea

No. I

The Palaces

Walk the throne halls of the Joseon dynasty beneath painted eaves of emerald and gold, where the changing of the guard still keeps a five-century rhythm.

No. II

Celadon & Lacquer

The jade-green glaze that emperors prized, and the mother-of-pearl inlay that takes a hundred patient coats — Korea's quiet, luminous craft tradition.

No. III

The Mountains

Seventy percent of the peninsula rises into mist-wrapped peaks, where granite temples and autumn maples reward those who climb to meet them.

No. IV

The Banquet

A royal table set in a dozen small jeweled dishes at once — a feast composed like a piece of music, each plate in balance with the next.

No. V

The Night Cities

Seoul and Busan after dark — neon over the river, rooftop tea houses, and night markets glittering like a jazz-age boulevard that never quite closes.

No. VI

The Hospitality

A grace the Koreans call jeong — the warmth that gathers between host and guest, offered to every traveler who arrives ready to receive it.
